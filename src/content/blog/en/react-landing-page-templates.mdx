Mercy of the devotees!

As for Hiraṇyakaśipu, although he was extremely inimical toward the Supreme Personality of Godhead, he always thought of his son, who was a great devotee. Therefore by the grace of his son, Prahlāda Mahārāja, Hiraṇyakaśipu was also delivered by the Supreme Personality of Godhead.

Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 7.1.26 Purport

There is a principle woven throughout the entire Vedic tradition — a truth so powerful and so beautiful that it can change the course of a person's spiritual life:

**The mercy of a devotee can save anyone.**
Even the most sinful.
Even the most rebellious.
Even the most hostile toward God.

And one of the most extraordinary examples of this truth is found in the history of **Hiraṇyakaśipu** and his saintly son **Prahlāda Mahārāja**.

The Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam states:

## **📜 The Verse (Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 7.1.26)**

```plain
hiraṇyakaśipuś cāpi
bhagavan-nindayā tamaḥ
vivakṣur atyagāt sūnoḥ
prahlādasyānubhāvataḥ
```

###

### **Translation:**

> **“As for Hiraṇyakaśipu, although he was extremely inimical toward the Supreme Personality of Godhead, he always thought of his son, who was a great devotee. Therefore, by the grace of his son, Prahlāda Mahārāja, Hiraṇyakaśipu was also delivered by the Supreme Personality of Godhead.”**

### **Purport by Srila Prabhupāda:**

In his purport, Srila Prabhupāda explains:

> **“Although Hiraṇyakaśipu constantly blasphemed the Supreme Lord, because he was always thinking of his son Prahlāda Mahārāja, a pure devotee, he became purified by the Lord’s mercy. This shows that the Lord is so kind that if someone is related to a devotee, even indirectly, they can become liberated.”**

## 🌺 **What a Profound Truth**

Hiraṇyakaśipu was the embodiment of atheism.
He tried to kill Prahlāda in countless ways.
He hated Vishnu so intensely that he shook the entire universe.

Yet… he was saved.

Not because he changed.
Not because he surrendered.
Not because he chanted.
Not because he repented.

But because **his son loved the Lord**.

This is the astonishing power of a devotee:
**Their devotion spills onto others — even those who oppose them.**

## 🌱 **Devotee Mercy Is Greater Than Any Karma**

Throughout his teachings, Srila Prabhupāda repeatedly emphasized that **the mercy of a pure devotee can override all karmic reactions**.

He said in a lecture:

> **“The devotee’s mercy is so great that even if one has no qualification, he becomes qualified simply by the devotee’s desire.”**

This is why Prahlāda could save his father.
His heart was so pure, his compassion so deep, that even a violent demon was touched by his devotion.

## 🔱 **Krishna Honors His Devotees’ Wish**

Krishna loves His devotees so much that He fulfills their desires — especially their desire to save others.

Srila Prabhupāda wrote:

> **“The Lord always wants to fulfill the desire of a devotee. Therefore, although Hiraṇyakaśipu was a great offender, the Lord delivered him because Prahlāda Mahārāja desired his liberation.”**

This is why Prahlāda prayed not for revenge, but for mercy.
Not for justice, but for compassion.
Not for punishment, but for liberation.

Krishna accepted his prayer.

## 🌸 **What This Means for Us Today**

This story is not mythology.
It is a message to every spiritual seeker:

- **Stay close to devotees.**
- **Serve devotees.**
- **Hear from devotees.**
- **Remember devotees.**

Because even if we are struggling, confused, or weak in spiritual life, **Krishna helps those who are connected to His devotees.**

Srila Prabhupāda said:

> **“If someone even slightly associates with a devotee, he becomes perfect. This is the mercy of the devotee.”**

This mercy is flowing here in Montreal too — through the devotees who chant, teach, serve, preach, and give their time and hearts for the pleasure of Krishna.

## 🌺 **Conclusion**

The story of Hiraṇyakaśipu is not about a demon.
It is about **mercy**, **grace**, and **the astonishing power of devotion**.

Prahlāda’s purity saved a soul who did not deserve saving.

And this is the hope Srila Prabhupāda gave the world:
**No one is too fallen.**
**No one is too late.**
**No one is beyond mercy.**

A single devotee can change everything.
