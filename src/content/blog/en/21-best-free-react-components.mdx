### harer nāma harer nāma

harer nāmaiva kevalam
kalau nāsty eva nāsty eva
nāsty eva gatir anyathā\*\*

> **“In this age of quarrel and hypocrisy, the only means of deliverance is the chanting of the holy name of the Lord. There is no other way, no other way, no other way.”**
> — _Bṛhan-nāradīya Purāṇa, quoted in Cc. Ādi 17.21_

Srila Prabhupāda often said that this verse is the **core instruction** for Kali-yuga.

He taught

**“Simply chant Hare Krishna. This is the only means for self-realization in this age. There is no other alternative.”**
— _Lecture on Cc. Ādi 17.21, Mayapur, 1976_

There is a moment in every temple gathering when the mridanga begins to pulse, the kartals begin to sing, and the devotees raise their voices together. The world outside fades away. Time slows down. And suddenly, everyone in the room is united in one simple, powerful act — **chanting the holy name**.

This is kīrtana.
And for many, it is the doorway into a life of devotion.

Srila Prabhupāda often explained that kīrtana is not merely music or ritual. In a lecture in London in 1969, he said:

> **“This chanting of Hare Krishna is not a material vibration. It is the spiritual sound that comes directly from the spiritual world.”**

This sound vibration is not different from Krishna Himself. The mantra carries His presence, His mercy, and His purifying power.

## 🌺 **Kīrtana Cleanses the Heart**

The Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam explains the effect of chanting beautifully:

> **“Sri Krishna, the Personality of Godhead, who is the Paramātmā in everyone’s heart… cleanses the desire for material enjoyment from the heart of the devotee who relishes His messages.”**
> — _SB 1.2.17, Translation_

Although this verse refers to hearing, Srila Prabhupāda often stated that chanting and hearing the holy name have **the same cleansing effect**.

In a morning walk in 1974, he said:

> **“Simply by chanting Hare Krishna, all impurities are washed away. This is the great benefit of this movement.”**

The heart becomes light, peaceful, and spiritually awake.

## 🌱 **Chanting Together Creates Powerful Spiritual Unity**

When devotees chant together, something profound happens. Srila Prabhupāda described it like this:

> **“When many devotees chant together, the vibration becomes very powerful. It is not ordinary sound. It is the sound that can awaken love of God.”**
> — _Lecture, New York, 1966_

This shared chanting dissolves differences — background, language, nationality, age, everything.
In that room, in that moment, everyone is simply a servant of Krishna.

## 🔱 **Kīrtana Is the Yuga-dharma — the Practice for This Age**

Kali-yuga is a time of confusion and suffering. But Lord Caitanya brought a gift that is perfect for this age.

The Caitanya-caritāmṛta states:

> **“In this age of quarrel and hypocrisy, the only means of deliverance is the chanting of the holy name of the Lord.”**
> — _CC Adi 17.21, Translation_

This is why ISKM centers around the world — including Montreal — place such importance on kīrtana, harinam, and bhajans.

## 💛 **Kīrtana Softens the Heart and Awakens Devotion**

Srila Prabhupāda once said:

> **“Chant Hare Krishna and feel ecstasy. This is a fact. If you chant sincerely, you will feel enthusiasm, purification, and joy.”**
> — _Lecture, Tokyo, 1972_

Many devotees describe their first kīrtana as life-changing.
Some felt peace for the first time in years.
Some felt relief from anxiety.
Some felt tears.
Some felt nothing — until months later, when suddenly their heart opened.

The holy name works silently, subtly, patiently.

## 🌸 **Conclusion: Let the Holy Name Enter Your Heart**

Kīrtana is not performance.
It is not entertainment.
It is not culture.

It is **Krishna in sound form**, entering the heart and awakening our forgotten love.

Come let your heart breathe again.

The holy name is waiting for you.
