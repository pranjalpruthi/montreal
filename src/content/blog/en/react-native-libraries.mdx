### _Based on the teachings of Srila Prabhupada_

**Gita Jayanti** is the sacred day commemorating the moment when **Lord Krishna spoke the Bhagavad-gita to Arjuna**on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. It is not only a historical event but a spiritual turning point for all humanity. Srila Prabhupada repeatedly emphasized that the Gita is **“the perfect instruction manual for human life”** (Lecture, BG 4.1, 1968).

## **1. What Is Gita Jayanti?**

According to the Vedic calendar, Gita Jayanti falls on the **Shukla Ekadashi of the month of Margashirsha**, the very day Krishna enlightened Arjuna with the timeless wisdom of the Gita.

Srila Prabhupada called the Gita **“the essence of all Vedic knowledge”** (Introduction to Bhagavad-gita As It Is).
This day invites us to reflect on Krishna’s teachings and renew our commitment to spiritual life.

## **2. Why Is the Bhagavad-gita Important?**

### **It explains the purpose of life**

Prabhupada said that the Gita teaches us **“why we are suffering and how to become happy”** (Interview, London, 1973).

### **It gives practical spiritual guidance**

He often explained that the Gita is not theoretical.
It is a “_handbook for everyday living_” (Letter, 1971), guiding us in devotion, duties, relationships, and inner peace.

### **It reveals Krishna as the Supreme Person**

Prabhupada emphasized that the Gita is Krishna’s direct words—**“not a commentary… Krishna Himself is speaking”** (Lecture, BG 7.1, 1966).
This makes the Gita unique among all scriptures.

## **3. Gita Jayanti is a Time to Reconnect**

### **A day to revisit Krishna’s instructions**

Prabhupada encouraged devotees to read the Gita “_regularly, daily_” (Morning Walk, 1975).
Gita Jayanti gives us a chance to renew that commitment.

### **A day to chant with deeper attention**

He explained that chanting the holy name is the essence of the Gita’s teaching—surrendering to Krishna through devotion (SB 1.2.6 Purport).

### **A day to share the Gita with others**

Prabhupada joyfully stressed distributing the Gita, calling it “_the greatest welfare work_” (Conversation, 1976).
Sharing even one Gita can transform someone’s life.

## **4. How to Celebrate Gita Jayanti**

- **Read a chapter or the whole Gita**
- **Participate in kirtan and yajna**
- **Distribute Bhagavad-gitas**
- **Offer prayers to Krishna and Arjuna**
- **Reflect on how to apply the Gita in daily life**

Prabhupada said that sincerely reading the Gita “_awakens spiritual understanding_” (Lecture, BG 2.13, 1966).

## **Conclusion: A Day of Spiritual Awakening**

Gita Jayanti reminds us that Krishna’s voice still speaks through the Bhagavad-gita.
Its wisdom is timeless, universal, and life-changing.

Let us honor this sacred day by hearing, chanting, and sharing Krishna’s divine message.
